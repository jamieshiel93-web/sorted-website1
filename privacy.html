02

PERSONAL INFORMATION

Information we may collect

The personal information we collect will depend on how you contact us and which services you ask us to provide.

We may collect:

  • Your name, address, email address and telephone number.
  • Your preferred method of communication.
  • Details of your enquiry and the services you are interested in.
  • Information about your home, routine, preferences and support requirements.
  • Appointment, visit and scheduling information.
  • Emergency contact details where appropriate.
  • Access instructions, alarm information or key-holding records where required for an agreed service.
  • Details concerning pets, contractors, household arrangements or property visits.
  • Records of correspondence, consultations, service reviews, complaints and feedback.
  • Payment and transaction records.
  • Information needed for invoicing, accounting and record keeping.
  • Website information such as your IP address, browser type, device information and pages visited, where collected through our hosting or website security systems.

We do not normally need to store complete debit or credit card details. Where card payments are available, payment information may be processed securely by the relevant payment provider.

03

COLLECTION

How we collect your information

We may collect information directly from you when you:

  • Complete a website enquiry form.
  • Contact us by telephone, email, WhatsApp or social media.
  • Book a consultation or appointment.
  • Request a quotation or service plan.
  • Complete a client form or service agreement.
  • Use one of our services.
  • Make a payment.
  • Provide feedback or make a complaint.

We may also receive information from a family member, representative, attorney, carer, professional or other person arranging support on your behalf. Where appropriate, we will confirm relevant information directly with you.

Limited technical information may also be collected automatically when you use our website.

04

PURPOSES

How we use your information

We may use personal information to:

  • Respond to enquiries and consultation requests.
  • Understand your requirements and assess how we can help.
  • Prepare quotations, proposals and personalised support plans.
  • Provide and manage agreed services.
  • Arrange appointments, visits, collections and errands.
  • Communicate with you about your service.
  • Contact an authorised representative or emergency contact.
  • Manage property access, key holding and home visits.
  • Process payments and issue invoices.
  • Maintain accurate business, financial and service records.
  • Manage safeguarding, security, incidents and complaints.
  • Improve our services and client experience.
  • Protect our clients, workers, business and property.
  • Meet legal, insurance, tax and regulatory responsibilities.
  • Prevent or investigate fraud, misuse or unlawful activity.
05

UK GDPR

Our lawful bases

Data protection law requires us to have a lawful reason for using your personal information. The basis we rely on depends on why we are using the information.

Purpose Information used Lawful basis
Responding to enquiries Name, contact details and information contained in your enquiry Legitimate interests and steps requested before entering into a contract
Providing agreed services Contact details, service requirements, schedules, property and access information Performance of a contract
Payments and accounting Contact, invoice, payment and transaction information Performance of a contract and legal obligation
Service administration and improvement Correspondence, service records, feedback and complaint information Legitimate interests
Security, safeguarding and incident management Contact, visit, incident and relevant support information Legitimate interests, legal obligation or vital interests, depending on the circumstances
Optional marketing Name, email address, telephone number and marketing preferences Consent or legitimate interests where legally permitted

Where we rely on legitimate interests, we consider whether using the information is necessary and whether your rights and interests override our business need.

Where we rely on consent, you may withdraw your consent at any time.

06

SENSITIVE INFORMATION

Health and other special-category information

In some circumstances, you may choose to tell us limited information about your health, disability, mobility, dietary requirements or other circumstances so we can provide suitable and safe support.

We will only request or use this type of information where it is relevant and necessary. We will identify both an appropriate lawful basis and an additional condition for processing special-category information where required.

Depending on the circumstances, this may include explicit consent, protecting vital interests, establishing or defending legal claims, or another condition permitted by data protection law.

Important

Sorted. does not provide personal care, nursing care, medical treatment or clinical advice. Please do not provide detailed medical records unless we have specifically explained why limited information is required.

07

DATA SHARING

Who we may share information with

We do not sell your personal information.

Where necessary and appropriate, information may be shared with:

  • Workers, assistants or contractors involved in providing your agreed service.
  • Your authorised family member, representative, attorney or emergency contact.
  • Contractors, tradespeople, delivery providers or other third parties you have asked us to coordinate with.
  • Website hosting, email, cloud storage, appointment-booking, accounting or payment providers.
  • Professional advisers, accountants, insurers or legal representatives.
  • Police, emergency services, safeguarding bodies, regulators, courts or public authorities where disclosure is required or permitted by law.

Where another company processes personal information on our behalf, we expect it to use appropriate security and confidentiality measures and only process information in accordance with our instructions.

08

INTERNATIONAL SERVICES

Transfers outside the United Kingdom

Some of the technology providers used to operate our website, email, bookings, cloud storage or business systems may process information outside the United Kingdom.

Where personal information is transferred internationally, we will take reasonable steps to ensure that a lawful transfer mechanism and suitable safeguards are in place. This may include an adequacy decision, recognised contractual protections or another mechanism permitted under data protection law.

09

PROTECTION

How we keep information secure

We use reasonable organisational and technical measures designed to protect personal information against unauthorised access, loss, disclosure, alteration or misuse.

These measures may include:

  • Password-protected accounts and devices.
  • Restricted access to client information.
  • Secure cloud services and account authentication.
  • Confidentiality requirements.
  • Secure handling of keys and access information.
  • Appropriate disposal or deletion of records.
  • Regular review of the information we retain.

No method of electronic storage or communication can be guaranteed to be completely secure. We nevertheless take data security seriously and will respond appropriately if we become aware of a personal-data breach.

10

RETENTION

How long we keep information

We keep personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, including to meet legal, tax, accounting, insurance and dispute-resolution requirements.

Type of record Usual retention approach
Unsuccessful or general enquiries Normally retained for up to 12 months after the last meaningful contact, unless there is a reason to keep it longer
Client and service records Normally retained for the duration of the client relationship and for up to 6 years afterwards
Invoices, payments and accounting records Normally retained for at least 6 years where required for tax and accounting purposes
Complaints, incidents or legal correspondence Retained for as long as reasonably necessary in light of the circumstances and any applicable limitation period
Marketing preferences Retained until you unsubscribe or object, with a limited suppression record kept where necessary to respect your choice
Keys and access information Retained only while required for the agreed service and securely returned, deleted or updated when no longer needed

These periods are general guidelines. We may retain information for a shorter or longer period where reasonably necessary or legally required.

YOUR CHOICES

Your data-protection rights

Depending on the circumstances and the lawful basis being used, you may have the following rights:

Right of access

You may ask for confirmation of whether we hold your personal information and request a copy.

Right to rectification

You may ask us to correct information that is inaccurate or complete information that is incomplete.

Right to erasure

In certain circumstances, you may ask us to delete your personal information.

Right to restrict processing

You may ask us to limit how we use your information in certain circumstances.

Right to data portability

In certain circumstances, you may ask for information you supplied to be provided in a structured, commonly used format.

Rights concerning automated decisions

You may have rights where a significant decision is made solely by automated means. Sorted. does not currently use solely automated decision-making for client services.

Right to withdraw consent

Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time.

Right to complain

You may raise concerns with us and may also complain to the Information Commissioner’s Office.

Your right to object

You have the right to object to certain uses of your personal information, including processing based on legitimate interests and the use of your information for direct marketing. Where you object to direct marketing, we will stop using your information for that purpose.

To exercise a right, contact us at contact@sortedlife.org.uk .

We may need to request information to confirm your identity before responding. Data-protection rights are not absolute, and an exemption may sometimes apply. We will explain our decision where we cannot fully comply with a request.

WEBSITE

Cookies and website information

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website.

Our website may use essential cookies or local browser storage where needed to operate correctly, remember your cookie-banner choice, provide security or support basic functionality.

Essential cookies do not normally require consent. We will not intentionally use optional analytics, advertising or marketing cookies without providing appropriate information and, where legally required, obtaining your consent first.

You can manage or delete cookies through your browser settings. Blocking essential cookies may affect how some website features operate.
